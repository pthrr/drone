# AT86RF215 Shield — Pin Mapping & Connections
# For Olimex iCE40HX8K-EVB (Rev B1)
# FPGA: iCE40-HX8K in CT256 BGA (same die as Lattice board)
# Pin numbers verified against AT86RF215 datasheet Table 2-1
# DP pair assignments derived from icestorm chipdb-8k tile structure

================================================================================
SPI INTERFACE (via EXT1, Bank 0)
================================================================================
Signal       EXT1 Pin  FPGA Ball  FPGA PIO         AT86RF215 Pin
--------------------------------------------------------------------
SPI_SCLK     13        B14        PIO0_03          41
SPI_MOSI     21        A16        PIO0_07          43
SPI_MISO     23        A15        PIO0_08          44
SPI_CSn      25        B13        PIO0_09          42 (SELN)
RF_IRQ       33        B12        PIO0_13          40
RF_RSTn      35        B10        PIO0_14          13 (RSTN)

EXT1 power: pin 1 = +3V3, pin 2 = GND

================================================================================
LVDS I/Q INTERFACE (via EXT4, Bank 3)
================================================================================

The lower Bank 3 pins (PIO3_00-PIO3_28, DP00-DP14, balls B2-J2) are used
by the Olimex on-board SRAM and are NOT available on any EXT header.

The upper Bank 3 pins (PIO3_29-PIO3_51, DP15-DP25) are on EXT4 and have
full differential pair capability. 11 DP pairs available, 4 needed.

Signal       EXT4 Pin  FPGA Ball  FPGA PIO/DP              AT86RF215 Pin  Term
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RXDP09       29        K1         PIO3_30/DP15A            31             R1
RXDN09       31        K3         PIO3_31/DP15B            30             R1
RXCLKP       33        L4         PIO3_32/DP16A            34             R2
RXCLKN       35        L1         PIO3_33/DP16B            33             R2
TXDP         37        K4         PIO3_34/DP17A            28             R3
TXDN         39        M1         PIO3_35/DP17B            27             R3
TXCLKP       40        L6         PIO3_36/DP18A            26             R4
TXCLKN       38        L3         PIO3_37/DP18B            25             R4

EXT4 power: pin 1 = +3V3, pin 2 = GND

Notes on LVDS:
  - RX pairs (DP15, DP16): true LVDS input buffers in iCE40 Bank 3.
    iCE40 HX8K supports up to 4 simultaneous LVDS inputs per bank.
  - TX pairs (DP17, DP18): pseudo-differential output (two LVCMOS, one inverted).
    Any I/O pair works; DP designation not required but helps routing.
  - At 400 kHz I/Q sample rate, LVDS clock is ~200 kHz (DDR).
    Signal integrity over pin headers is not a concern at these frequencies.
  - All 4 pairs are in a contiguous block (EXT4 pins 29-40) for clean routing.

================================================================================
POWER
================================================================================

+3.3V from Olimex board (via EXT1 pin 1 or EXT4 pin 1)
  |
  +---> AT86RF215 EVDD (pins 15, 46) -- external analog supply
  |     +-- C1, C2 (100nF) to GND
  |
  +---> AT86RF215 DEVDD (pins 21, 29, 39) -- external digital supply
  |     +-- C3, C4, C5 (100nF) to GND
  |
  +---> TCXO VDD -- 3.3V
        +-- C11 (100nF) to GND

Internal regulated outputs (need decoupling caps only, no external LDO):
  AT86RF215 AVDD0 (pin 14) -- internal 1.8V analog sub-1GHz
        +-- C6 (100nF) to GND
  AT86RF215 AVDD1 (pin 47) -- internal 1.8V analog 2.4GHz
        +-- C7 (100nF) to GND
  AT86RF215 DVDD (pin 37) -- internal 1.8V digital
        +-- C8 (100nF) to GND
        +-- C9 (10uF) bulk to GND

Note: EVDD and DEVDD must be shorted at PCB level (same 3.3V rail).
      AVDD0, AVDD1, DVDD must NOT be shorted -- separate internal regulators.

================================================================================
RF PATH (sub-GHz only)
================================================================================

Sub-GHz (432 MHz / 868 MHz):
  AT86RF215 RFP09 (pin 8) --> C12 (DC block) --> L1/C13 (LC match to 50R) --> SMA J5
  AT86RF215 RFN09 (pin 9) --> C14 (100pF) --> GND (terminated)
  Single-ended drive: ~3 dB loss, 30 dB margin remains

2.4 GHz frontend NOT USED -- RFP24 (pin 4) and RFN24 (pin 5) left floating.
FEA24 (pin 1), FEB24 (pin 2) no-connect.

Antenna: single dual-band whip for 432 + 868 MHz (2:1 frequency ratio).
  LC match designed for broadband; 2-3 dB mismatch loss at off-center
  frequency is negligible with 30+ dB margin on both bands.

Note: No balun needed -- link budget has >30 dB margin.
      Match values per AT86RF215 datasheet single-ended application note.
      Verify with VNA on actual PCB.

================================================================================
LVDS TERMINATION
================================================================================

100R resistors placed CLOSE TO FPGA SIDE (receiver end):

  R1: RXDP09 --[100R]-- RXDN09   (sub-GHz RX data)
  R2: RXCLKP --[100R]-- RXCLKN   (RX clock)

100R resistors placed CLOSE TO AT86RF215 SIDE (receiver end):

  R3: TXDP   --[100R]-- TXDN     (TX data, AT86RF215 receives)
  R4: TXCLKP --[100R]-- TXCLKN   (TX clock, AT86RF215 receives)

================================================================================
RESET CIRCUIT
================================================================================

                +3.3V
                  |
                  R5 (10K)
                  |
  FPGA PIO0_14 --+---- AT86RF215 RSTN (pin 13)
                  |
                 C18 (100nF)
                  |
                 GND

R5 holds RSTN high (active). FPGA can pull low to reset.
C18 provides power-on reset delay (~1ms with 10K).

================================================================================
26 MHz TCXO
================================================================================

  Y1 (26MHz TCXO, +/-2ppm)
    OUT --> AT86RF215 TCXO (pin 17)
    VDD --> +3.3V via C11 (100nF)
    GND --> GND

  AT86RF215 XTAL2 (pin 18) --> GND (required in TCXO mode)

================================================================================
ACTIVE-LOW ACCENT
================================================================================

  SELN (pin 42): pull-up R6 (10K) to +3.3V -- deselects SPI when FPGA not driving
  RSTN (pin 13): pull-up R5 (10K) to +3.3V -- keeps chip out of reset

================================================================================
PCB LAYOUT NOTES
================================================================================

1. 4-layer stackup recommended: SIG-GND-PWR-SIG
2. LVDS pairs: route as 100R differential, length-matched within pair
3. RF traces: 50R microstrip, keep as short as possible
4. AT86RF215 exposed pad: solid GND via array
5. Separate AVSS and DVSS ground pours, connect at single point under IC
6. Place decoupling caps within 1mm of IC power pins
7. Shield outline matches Olimex iCE40HX8K-EVB board dimensions
8. Stacking headers: use extra-long-pin female headers (e.g. Samtec SSW series)
   Shield plugs into EXT1 (SPI) and EXT4 (LVDS) on the Olimex board
9. SMA connector on board edge
10. Keep LVDS traces away from RF section
11. EXT1 and EXT4 are on opposite sides of the Olimex board --
    shield must span the full board width or use a flex cable for one connector

================================================================================
COMPONENT REFERENCE CHANGES (vs. original Lattice-targeted design)
================================================================================

Removed (2.4GHz not used):
  R2 (RXDP24/RXDN24 term) -- was 100R
  J6 (SMA 2.4GHz) -- was SMA_EDGE
  C15, L2, C16 (2.4GHz match) -- were TBD/0402
  C17 (RFN24 term) -- was 100pF

Renumbered:
  R1: RXDP09/RXDN09 termination (100R) -- unchanged
  R2: RXCLKP/RXCLKN termination (100R) -- was R3
  R3: TXDP/TXDN termination (100R) -- was R4
  R4: TXCLKP/TXCLKN termination (100R) -- was R5
  R5: RSTN pull-up (10K) -- was R6
  R6: SELN pull-up (10K) -- was R7

Header connectors:
  J3: 2x20 header → mates with Olimex EXT1 (was "iCE40 J2")
  J4: 2x20 header → mates with Olimex EXT4 (was "iCE40 J4")

================================================================================
HOST INTERFACE (not on the RF shield — separate wiring)
================================================================================

Arduino Uno Q connects to Olimex iCE40 via SPI data bus on EXT2 or EXT3.
Both boards are 3.3V logic — no level shifting required.
This connection is NOT routed through the RF shield board.

Three separate SPI domains in the system:
  1. Config SPI:  FPGA master → AT86RF215 slave    (shield EXT1, Bank 0)
  2. Data SPI:    Uno Q master → FPGA slave         (EXT2/3, Bank 0/1)
  3. Flash SPI:   FTDI → SPI flash → iCE40          (on-board, not on headers)

Programming workflow:
  - FPGA bitstream: iceprog via Olimex USB/FTDI → on-board SPI flash
  - iCE40 auto-loads from flash on every power cycle
  - Arduino firmware: USB-C on Uno Q board
  - AT86RF215 config: FPGA writes registers via SPI at boot + band switch
